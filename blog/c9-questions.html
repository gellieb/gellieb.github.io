Hmm.. That's a Good Question!

February 22, 2015

When learning a new concept in any field, you're bound to have questions. We can easily find answers to something we're looking for by typing in a couple keywords, but that's not the same thing as asking a question. When asking a question, whether to a friend, collegue, professor, or anyone, you want to phrase your question well. Especially when asking a question on Google+ or StackOverFlow. Since you're posting your question and can't really elaborate further as you would in person, you should do the following things:

+ Write a title that aptly summarizes your problem.
Imagine as if you're catching your professor as he's rushing off to his next lecture, sum up your entire question in a single sentence. You want to responder to understand what exactly is the problem.

+ Spell and grammar check.
I don't want to read gibberish, neither does anyone else.

+ Expand more
After your very clear title, expand more in depthly in a paragraph or not. Keep it relevant and informative.

+ Make sure you're adding screenshots of error messages, or including a snippet of what you're working on. Do this so that someone can reproduce the problem and hopefully solve it.

+ Include all relevant tags.
Tags for: language, specific API, library, etc.

+ Post your question and respond to feedback.
People may want you to elaborate on a certain portion of your problem or to check if their solution/feedback ameliorated the problem.
